1 # Gossip Protocol assistant notes
2
3 Gossip Protocol is a Rust reference implementation for peer-witnessed public
4 infrastructure. It coordinates checks, browser journeys, compute jobs, storage
5 contracts, domain routes, swaps, and public outputs through signed receipts.
6
7 The codebase is intentionally the source of truth. Keep explanations brief,
8 prefer file references and local commands, and do not make operational claims
9 that are not backed by this repository.
10
11 ## Current shape
12
13 - Binary/crate: `gossip_protocol`
14 - Public site: `site/`
15 - License: Apache-2.0
16 - Core roles: validators, probe/compute agents, wallets, storage hosts
17 - Lifecycle: submit, witness, settle, publish
18 - Ledger tokens:
19 - `HT`: health checks, browser checks, monitor budgets, swaps
20 - `CT`: distributed compute jobs
21 - `ST`: storage contracts and storage proofs
22 - `DNS`: domain offerings, leases, renewals, and binds
23 - User-provided arbitrary compute is WASI-module based. Larger modules should
24 use storage-addressed `module_ref` rather than large inline `module_hex`.
25
26 ## Safe defaults
27
28 - Prefer encrypted wallets.
29 - Do not invent live peers, API tokens, genesis files, validator policies, or
30 production onboarding requirements.
31 - Treat live validator participation as operator-controlled until the repo says
32 otherwise.
33 - Use local smokes for confidence before making release claims.

74 ## Brief architecture
75
76 Validators gossip transactions, receipts, proofs, leases, and block approvals.
77 Agents execute delegated HTTP, browser, and compute work. The ledger finalizes
78 results only after enough valid receipts reach consensus. Storage and DNS are
79 first-class protocol surfaces rather than side utilities.
80
81 When explaining compute, emphasize embarrassingly parallel workloads: Monte
82 Carlo, batch transforms, static analysis, bounded WASI modules, shardable
83 inference, media processing, and proof generation.
84
85 When explaining the whole project, do not over-index on compute. The core claim
86 is one receipt and settlement layer for many resource markets.
